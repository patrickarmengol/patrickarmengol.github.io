Coffee in Orlando

2023-09-25

Note: These are the opinions of an idiot that knows a tiny bit about coffee and has very particular preferences.

Orlando, FL

Orlando is my home town. It’s where I grew up. It’s where I went to university. It’s where my parents live.

Orlando sucks. It’s a perpetually hot and humid, sprawly, stroad-ridden mess, filled with insane/old people. It’s a typical, car-centric, sun-belt city and the only thing it seems to have going for it is tourism. And not the interesting kind of tourism, where you hike up a volcano, cycle along a canal, try local cuisine, explore the remains of a dilapidated castle, or even just meet people at a hostel and hang out downtown. It’s the boring kind of tourism with planned fun at a “variety” of expensive amusement parks where you wait an hour in line for a 15 second adrenaline rush, and then walk around a simulation of a walkable European town to buy overpriced food and toys.

I find myself in circumstances where I have little choice but to live temporarily in this suburban hell hole that everyone but myself seems to enjoy so much. To escape from my room, I usually like to visit coffee shops to see if the experience compares to what I am used to in other cities.

Coffee Options

Initially, I thought the options looked pretty bleak. Most of what’s around me is just drive-thru, american-style coffee that people add copious amounts of sweetened creamer to and chug for the sole purpose of waking themselves up in the morning.

I mean… I don’t think I’m a coffee snob. I just don’t understand why so many people are satisfied with the status-quo of coffee. Gone are the days of reliance on cans of stale, pre-ground coffee from the supermarket. The third wave has already hit most corners of the world and the information is available for people and businesses to see what can actually be done with coffee. It doesn’t even seem like a cost or availability issue.

Unfortunately, if you want the good stuff, your best bet is to buy it online with a subscription like Trade and have it shipped to your place to brew yourself.

But if you still want that chill coffee shop experience, you’ll have to explore a bit. With some trial and error, and enough effort, you can discover the some cool spots.

Below are the results of my search. I feel like I gave each of these a fair shake, visiting most of them at least twice and trying different items. I tried to look for coffee-focused spots, rather than food-focused spots that also served coffee. They are grouped by judgement but listed in no particular order.
