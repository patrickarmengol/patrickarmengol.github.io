Europe Bicycle Tour 2022

2022-08-25

On 2022-04-19, I started a journey, cycling from my sister’s place in Barcelona, all the way to Helsinki.

Why?

Why did I do it? Hmmm… I’m not too sure. Over the last couple years, I had been pining for some kind of grand journey, some way to travel that was unique and epic.

When I quit my job last year, I started thru-hiking the GR 11 trail in the Pyrenees. Unfortunately, I didn’t make it even half way, but it was a mindset altering experience. One foot in front of the other, carrying my own weight, making sure I had enough water and food and time to find a campsite, sleeping under the stars, smelling the fresh air, exercising every day, meeting all sorts of cool people. I ended up staying in Barcelona for quite a bit, thinking I might start living there. Still jobless and wanting for travel, I backpacked my way through Spain and Portugal for the rest of the year. In January, I made it back to Florida and started writing this blog and studying. But the itch to travel was lingering. I decided to extend my sabbatical.

I was inspired by some people on YouTube that documented their round-the-world bike touring and decided I wanted to try that form of travel. It seemed to strike a nice balance between long-distance hiking and city-hopping backpacking. All the benefits of being out in nature, seeing the lesser known rural areas, while also being fast enough to visit many interesting bigger cities.

Where did I go?

From Barcelona, I made my way through Catalunya and over the Pyrenees to Perpignan. At Narbonne, I turned West to follow the Canal des Deux Mers, but quickly doubled back because of the wind. I followed the Rhône all the way to Geneva, where I visited CERN. I read Jurassic Park as I cycled the Jura mountains. I stuck to the Rhine for a bit, before reaching Saarland. I rode through lovely Luxembourg and boring Belgium. The cycling infrastructure in the Netherlands lived up to the hype. Germany had me riding too many bumpy bricky sidewalks. I passed by the Tour de France in Denmark. Sweden showed me some absolutely picturesque views, but gave me 2 cm of space on the shoulder of high speed roads. Cought covid-19 at a hostel in Stockholm and suffered the symptoms on one of the Åland islands. Bummer of an ending, but I caught a train to make it to Helsinki for a flight.

How did it go?

I’m absolutely glad that I did it. Sure, I wish I had done some things differently, and I definitely was too anxious about interacting with the locals, but I still had a life-changing experience and memories that will stay with me forever. Halfway through the trip I started to feel, uhmm… bored? I got into too much of a routine. Wake up, cycle, find coffee, cycle, find lunch, cycle, find dinner, cycle, find a place to camp, sleep, repeat. All I had for entertainment were my podcasts, which at least kept me sane, but I was missing something, probably meaningful human social interaction, maybe something mentally challenging. I wanted so much for the trip to be finished already, but I didn’t want to end my trip early. I wanted to start looking for work and living in an apartment and meeting up with friends and playing video games.

Would I do it again?

I would like to have the opportunity to do something like this again in the future. If I were to do another bicycle tour, it would probably be in Africa or the other parts of Europe.

What now?

I’m writing this blog post on my new laptop in my hotel room in Seoul. I came here so that I could live somewhat more comfortably and cheaply, in a kinda familiar place, where I can speak a bit of the language. My goal for the next few months is to study in preperation for a job search. I’m very worried that I will have trouble finding a dev job with no direct experiencee and after 1 year of not working. I have to try.
