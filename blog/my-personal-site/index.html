My Personal Site

2022-01-22

I want to write a bit about this site and why it exists.

Maybe two years ago, I decided to register the .com domain for my name, patrickarmengol.com. I found it available, despite at least a couple other “Patrick Armengol"s existing (one of them must have taken parmengol@gmail.com). I didn’t really plan to do anything with it.

I don’t know what happened, but soon afterwards I was motivated to make a website/blog. The only experience I had in making websites prior to this was playing with HTML when I was ~11 years old. After a bit of research, I chose to utilize Hugo to make a simple static site, and took advantage of free hosting on GitHub Pages. I had some fun trying out the themes, learning about file structure, and customizing the site to my liking.

Initially, I wanted the site to be like one of those cybersec blogs I perused at work. The plan was to show off my qualifications in an ‘online resume’ and post about interesting findings from my security research. That was abruptly halted when I realized I could get in legal trouble with my employer depending on what I posted. That, and I didn’t really have much to write about or the courage to write it. So the site ended up being an empty professional profile; not really something I would want to show off. The site sat idle for a while.

Later, I decided to scrap my plans and instead turn the site into something more personal, not professional. It really just housed my resource lists (a bunch of bookmark links) for stuff I was interested in. Unexpectedly, around this time, one of my highschool friends found my site and reconnected via email. The site then sat idle for an even longer while.

A few weeks ago, when I set my desktop PC back up, I felt like I wanted to try again. I changed the theme to Hugo Bear Blog and switched hosting to GitLab Pages. I’m not entirely sure what I want out of the site yet, but I do know I need to be less afraid of posting to the blog. I suck at writing. I spend way too much time deliberating over what words to use, and still end up sounding unnatural. So far, I’ve only posted my learning plans for python and Japanese. Ideas for future posts include: learning plans for other subjects, progress updates for the things I’m learning, reviews for books, reviews for games, reviews for resources/tools, instructional posts on obscure shit, and details on my projects. My hope is that writing these things out publicly will make me a better writer, let me organize my thoughts, help me remember things, and document my journeys. It might even serve as a source of motivation.

I don’t expect anyone other than myself to actually read my posts. In fact, I think I would feel quite embarrassed if I found out anyone did. But that won’t stop me. Let’s see how it goes.
