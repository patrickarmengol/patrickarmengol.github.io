This is my short review of "Dive Into Algorithms" by Bradford Tuckfield. 

## About

This book is marketed as an intro to algorithms for beginners. 

## Things I liked

- real world relevance
- introduction to more advanced/specialized concepts
- math

## Things I disliked

- the author's writing style
- the author's coding style
- the author's deviation from PEP 8
- the exclusion of data structures
- non-comprehensivity (is that a word?) of which algorithms were introduced
- weirdly complex examples for beginners
- nested list comprehensions

## Conclusion

I was looking for a book that would help me refresh my knowledge of data structures and algorithms. This was not the right book for the job. This is more of an intro to why algorithms are useful. The author keeps droning on about how algorithms can be invented/discovered/improved to fulfill some real world purpose. I get it... I just want to learn how to invert a binary tree please. I want a primer that will let me understand the prompts of leetcode problems. 

On top of the mismatch with my intentions, the book was written in a bizzarely annoying fashion. The author uses silly words like 'hitherto'. Some examples use hard math that could make somebody's eyes glaze over and disuade them from reading further. The code is needlessly convoluted and variable names are poorly chosen. At one point, I just got so frustrated with the author's choices in the example code, that I decided to stop reading the book altogether. I almost never stop something midway, even shitty anime shows. I guess I'll have to find another resource.
