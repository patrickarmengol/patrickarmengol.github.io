I like Japan. I like Japanese [food](https://www.justonecookbook.com/ochazuke/), [comedy](https://www.reddit.com/r/GakiNoTsukai/wiki/nlbg), [anime](https://myanimelist.net/anime/486/Kino_no_Tabi__The_Beautiful_World), [music](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5W9S9la-C6s), [architecture](https://images.adsttc.com/media/images/51e2/ef0a/e8e4/4e7c/8800/00c5/large_jpg/CIMG5246.jpg?1373826813), [games](https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-video-games/pokemon-yellow-special-pikachu-edition/), and [tea](https://www.hibiki-an.com/index.php). Am I wierd for liking Japan? I don't think so; it's just _one_ of the places/cultures on this planet that interest me. 

I've been to Japan three times; the first for a weekend, then for 2 weeks, then for 2 months. Though I thoroughly enjoyed my time there, I wish I had visited more of the non-touristy bits. I also regret not being able to communicate in the local language. That 2 month stint working in an office in Kawasaki got me to at least survival Japanese on imersion alone, but I put in no additional effort. Adding Japanese to my language repertoire has been on my list of life goals for a while. 

So here I am... setting off on this endeavor (for the third time). Growing up in a multicultural family gave me at least functional knowledge of English, Spanish, Portuguese, and Catalan, and my time in Korea earned me intermediate level Korean. I know how tough language learning is and how quickly I can fall off. I need to be consistent, determined, focused, and patient. I'm not under any delusion that I will "become fluent in 3 months" like many online courses promise. Heck, it would probably take me at least 2 years for just N1. My goal for now is an intermediate level where I should be able to hold a basic conversation and understand most things that I read or listen to.
